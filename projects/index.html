Enhancing the interpretability of DL models for traffic forecast through visual analytics

Jingyan Li

Deep learning (DL) models have shown strong predictive power in solving traffic problems but been controversial in application due to their lack of interpretability and transparency. Even though explainable AI (XAI) approaches have been widely explored in vision or language models, few studies on traffic forecasts have emphasized the explainability of DL models. It poses potential risks for decision making especially when the model fails at some abnormal cases. In the study, we considered the inherent characteristics underdying traffic data - the spatiotemporal correlation of speed and volume, and proposed a pipeline to interpret how UNet, a winning DL model in the image-based short-term traffic forecast competition, makes decision through feature attribution methods in the emerging XAI field.

Simulating spreading of COVID-19 in Zurich: An application of EpiSim

Sebastiano Papini & Jingyan Li

In the face of COVID-19 pandemic, we are curious if we can simulate the spreading through people's travel behavior. In this project, we extended EpiSim, an agent-based model (ABM) for epidemic simulation, to fit the first COVID-19 wave in Zurich, then calibrated the ABM with real-time hospitalization rates as it is more previse than positive test rates. Different containment measurements were tested and proved to be effective.

Story Map: Towards an HIV-Free Generation

Han Sun & Jingyan Li

Good health and well being, as one of United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, shows the world pays special attention to reduce the infection and death rate of HIV/AIDS. We created a story map to visualize how the world makes efforts to achieve an HIV-free generation. The following topics are addressed: history and current situation of HIV worldwide, heterogenous distribution comparing Sub-Saharan Africa and Western Europe, progress we've made, and possible actions.

Compass app in Android

Jingyan Li

This is a location-based fitness app based on Android. The app aims to encourage users to get out of their home and do a round trip around their home. A reward will give to the user once he/she successfully finishes a round trip. By using the app, users are able to direct their ways by the compass, recording trajectories and locating their rewards in an AR scene. The app was developed by ArcGIS Runtime API for Android, Google AR API and Azure Spatial Anchors.

Transportation Planning Methods

Fabio M. Cachaco & Jingyan Li

This is a course project for Transportation Planing Methods. We used Mobidrive dataset to implement the transportation planing methods - descriptive analysis of citizens' mobility behavior, trip production, trip distribution by gravity model, mode choice by multinomial logit model and cost benefit analysis of a given planing policy.
