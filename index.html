Global Trade Trend in COVID-19

Jingyan Li & Yuanwen Yue

The COVID-19 pandemic represents an unprecedented disruption to the global economy and world trade. The production and consumption are scaled back across the globe. As a course project in Cartography Lab, we created a web-based story map to show how COVID-19 is changing the world from an economic perspective.

Towards an HIV-Free Generation

Jingyan Li & Han Sun

Good health and well being, as one of United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, shows the world pays special attention to reduce the infection and death rate of HIV/AIDS. As a course project in Multimedia Cartography, we created a story map to visualize how the world makes efforts to achieve an HIV-free generation. We addressed the following topics: history and current situation of HIV worldwide, heterogenous distribution comparing Sub-Saharan Africa and Western Europe, progress we've made, and possible actions.

Enhancing the interpretability of DL models for traffic forecast through visual analytics

Jingyan Li

Deep learning (DL) models have shown strong predictive power in solving traffic problems but been controversial in application due to their lack of interpretability and transparency. Even though explainable AI (XAI) approaches have been widely explored in vision or language models, few studies on traffic forecasts have emphasized the explainability of DL models. It poses potential risks for decision making especially when the model fails at some abnormal cases. In the study, we considered the inherent characteristics underdying traffic data - the spatiotemporal correlation of speed and volume, and proposed a pipeline to interpret how UNet, a winning DL model in the image-based short-term traffic forecast competition, makes decision through feature attribution methods in the emerging XAI field.
